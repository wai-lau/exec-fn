# Pollack's Tarot — The Celtic Cross

Ten cards in two structures — a cross of six on the left, a staff of four on the right — and the dialogue between them is the reading.

## The Cross (left side)

The cross is the **situation as it actually is now**.

1. **Heart of the Matter.** What is at the centre. Not what the querent says the question is, but what the cards say it really is. Every other card refers back to it.
2. **Crossing Influence.** Laid across the first. Not hostile by default — it is whatever is *cutting across* the heart, for good or ill. Always read paired with the Heart; the two together are the engine.
3. **Crown.** Above the Heart. What is conscious — what the querent knows, believes, or hopes openly.
4. **Foundation.** Below the Heart. What lies underneath, often unconscious. Compare Crown and Foundation: the gap between what is acknowledged and what is buried is often the reading's most important truth.
5. **Recent Past.** What has just moved out of the situation.
6. **Near Future.** What is moving in. A tendency, not a fixed prophecy.

## The Staff (right side)

Four cards stacked bottom to top, reading upward.

7. **The Querent.** How the querent stands now.
8. **Environment.** People, places, currents around them.
9. **Hopes and Fears.** Both at once — the things wanted so much they are also feared.
10. **Outcome.** The likely culmination if nothing about the trajectory changes.

## Pollack's Paired Readings

- **Heart x Crossing (1 x 2)** — the central tension. The engine.
- **Crown x Foundation (3 x 4)** — conscious vs unconscious. When they diverge, name the gap.
- **Recent Past x Near Future (5 x 6)** — direction of motion through time.
- **Hopes/Fears x Outcome (9 x 10)** — desire against likelihood. The most quietly powerful pair.
- **The staff as a whole (7 -> 10)** — the querent's vertical line through the situation.

## Reading the Whole Spread

1. **Count the Majors.** A Major-heavy spread is being driven by archetypal forces; a Minor-heavy spread is the everyday.
2. **Watch the suit balance.** The dominant suit is the room the reading is happening in.

Read in this order: Heart and Crossing first to find the engine; then the vertical (Crown above, Foundation below); then the horizontal (Past behind, Future ahead); then the staff bottom to top. The reading is finished when the pairs have spoken to each other, not when ten paragraphs have been recited.
