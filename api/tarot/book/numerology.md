# Numerology Across the Pips and Courts

The number a Minor Arcana card carries tells you almost as much as the suit. Combine these with the suit overview to read any pip or court card that doesn't have its own chapter.

## Pips

- **Ace** — Pure essence. The suit at its source: a gift, a beginning, an offer. Aces are potential, not yet committed.
- **Two** — Pairing, balance, choice. The first relation; two of something brought into a single frame. Often a poised moment before commitment.
- **Three** — First manifestation. The two becomes a third; the relation produces a result. Three brings something new into the world, often a gathering or a creation.
- **Four** — Stability and structure. The thing now has form. Four can mean security or stagnation; the question is whether the structure holds life or freezes it.
- **Five** — Disruption. The four cracks open. Five is conflict, loss, or change that breaks an established order. Necessary, often painful.
- **Six** — Restoration. After the five, a return to harmony. Healing, generosity, the easing of pressure. Six is rarely the end of the story but it is a place to rest.
- **Seven** — Inner challenge. A test, often solitary. Seven is reflection, temptation, the moment the journey turns inward. The querent is being asked something.
- **Eight** — Mastery in motion. Competence, skill, the suit being practised with confidence. Eight is movement and momentum.
- **Nine** — Fulfilment. The suit's promise nearly complete, for good or ill. Nines are vivid and saturated — the situation at full strength.
- **Ten** — Completion. The cycle finishes. Tens often push toward something new, since completion is also ending.

## Courts

- **Page** — The student. The suit met with openness, not yet mastered. A messenger or a beginner; the apprentice attitude.
- **Knight** — Active expression of the suit, often extreme. The Knight is single-minded — passionate, restless, sometimes reckless. The energy of the suit on horseback.
- **Queen** — Inward mastery. The suit held from the centre and lived from a place of integration. A quality of presence rather than action.
- **King** — Outward mastery, authority. The suit expressed with responsibility and command; the established figure of that element in the world.

## How to Read a Pip Card Without a Chapter

For any Minor Arcana card that doesn't have a dedicated chapter, combine the suit overview, the number's theme, and the orientation. *Eight of Wands* upright: mastery x fire x in motion → swift action, momentum, things in flight. *Five of Cups* reversed: disruption x feeling x inflected → grief that is internalised rather than expressed, or beginning to lift. The card's meaning is the texture of its number lived inside its suit, modulated by orientation and position.
