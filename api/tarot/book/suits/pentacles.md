Pentacles are the suit of earth — the body, work, money, and the slow real things of material life. Where Pentacles appear, the reading is about how a life is built: the job, the home, the apprenticeship, the patient craft, the bank account, the body's health. Pollack reads Pentacles as a sacred suit, not a mundane one: the work of hands, the discipline of years, the dignity of building something that lasts. The conflicts in Pentacles are about scarcity and security, but also about whether the material work is serving something larger or has become a cage. Pentacles ask: what are you actually building, and for whom?
