Cups are the suit of water — feeling, love, imagination, the inner life. Where Cups appear, the reading is about the heart's weather: closeness and distance, joy and grief, longing and satisfaction. Cups can mean visions, dreams, art, and the gentleness of relationship; they can also describe the soft erosions of disappointment, self-pity, or fantasy turned into avoidance. In Pollack's reading, Cups carry the truth of what a person actually feels, including the feelings they have not yet admitted. Their conflicts are not violent; they are tender, slow, and inward.
