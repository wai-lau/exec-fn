Wands are the suit of fire — will, drive, vitality, the will to act. Where Wands appear, something is being initiated, contested, or carried by force of personal energy. Wands describe projects and ambitions, creative work, rivalries, the heat of beginnings and the friction of ongoing campaigns. They are about doing, and about the doer. In Pollack's reading, Wands ask whether a person's energy is pointed at the right thing; whether the fire is illuminating or merely burning. Their conflicts are about ambition, struggle, and the question of which direction to throw oneself.
