# Pollack's Tarot — Core Framework

Background notes that distil Pollack's general framework before any specific reading.

## The Shape of the Deck

The seventy-eight cards split into two arcanas. The **Major Arcana** (twenty-two cards, 0–21) carries the archetypal current — the symbolic stages of a soul moving through experience. The **Minor Arcana** (fifty-six cards in four suits) describes the texture of ordinary life: events, feelings, projects, conflicts. The four **court cards** in each suit — Page, Knight, Queen, King — function as people or as facets of personality, not events.

A reading is the meeting of these registers. A Major card says something archetypal is at work; a Minor card describes the texture of how it lands.

## The Fool's Journey — Three Rows of the Major Arcana

Pollack arranges the Majors in three rows of seven, with the Fool (0) as the wanderer who steps into the sequence.

**Row One — the worldly sequence (1–7):** Magician, High Priestess, Empress, Emperor, Hierophant, Lovers, Chariot. The forming of an outward self. Conscious will pairs with unconscious knowing; mother and father are met as principles; the social form is given by the Hierophant; the first real choice arrives in the Lovers; the willed self heads out as the Chariot.

**Row Two — the inward turn (8–14):** Strength, the Hermit, the Wheel of Fortune, Justice, the Hanged Man, Death, Temperance. The ego yields. Strength tames the inner beast; the Hermit withdraws for inner light; the Wheel teaches that fate is larger than will; Justice asks for honest reckoning; the Hanged Man inverts perspective; Death ends the old self; Temperance mixes the new one.

**Row Three — the spiritual realisation (15–21):** the Devil, the Tower, the Star, the Moon, the Sun, Judgement, the World. The encounter with what lies past the personal. Chosen bondage, sudden destruction, gentle renewal, half-lit unconscious, conscious joy, the call to a new life, the integrated whole.

The Fool walks through all three rows. He is the openness that lets the journey happen.

## The Four Suits

- **Wands — Fire.** Will, drive, passion, projects. Where wands appear, something is being initiated or contested by force of personal energy.
- **Cups — Water.** Feeling, relationship, imagination. Cups describe what the heart is doing — joy, grief, longing, attachment.
- **Swords — Air.** Thought, language, struggle, the cutting clarity of the mind. The suit where suffering most often appears.
- **Pentacles — Earth.** Body, work, money, the material world. The slow real things of how a life is built.

## Numerology Across the Suits

The ten pip cards echo a number-pattern Pollack reads as a small Fool's journey of its own:

- **Ace** — pure essence; a gift, a seed.
- **Two** — pairing, balance, choice.
- **Three** — first manifestation.
- **Four** — stability, structure.
- **Five** — disruption, the four broken open.
- **Six** — restoration after the five.
- **Seven** — inner challenge, reflection.
- **Eight** — mastery in motion.
- **Nine** — fulfilment of the suit's promise.
- **Ten** — completion, often pushing toward the next thing.

## The Court Cards

Each suit has four court figures. Pollack treats them as both **people** and **facets** of the querent.

- **Page** — the student, the messenger; the suit met with openness, not yet mastered.
- **Knight** — active expression of the suit, often extreme.
- **Queen** — the suit held inwardly, integrated.
- **King** — the suit expressed outwardly with authority.

## Reversed Cards — Pollack's Position

A reversed card is the same archetypal energy as the upright, **inflected**: blocked, delayed, internalised, distorted, or showing the shadow side rather than the light. The upright meaning is the starting point even when the card is reversed — modulate, don't replace.

## How Pollack Reads

1. **The cards talk to each other.** A reading is a conversation between positions, not parallel one-card interpretations.
2. **Position changes meaning.** Read each card *through* its position.
3. **The querent is in the cards.** Honesty inside compassion; describe the situation as it is.
4. **Tarot reveals; it does not predict.** The future is a tendency.
5. **The Major Arcana raises the stakes.** A spread heavy in Majors is talking about deep currents; a spread of Minors is the everyday.
