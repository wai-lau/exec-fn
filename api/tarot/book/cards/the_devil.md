# The Devil (XV)

Why this grim figure so late? After Temperance's balance, why fall so abruptly? 15 reduces to 6 — Waite worked backwards from the Devil when redesigning the Lovers, and the Rider Devil is the Lovers' perversion. So why the "true" version early and the perversion near the end?

The Devil opens the last row. That position hints at the answer: he provides some vital energy for the work ahead. Dante goes through Hell before Purgatory and Paradise; Blake read Milton's Satan as the real hero of *Paradise Lost*. Enlightenment passes through here.

The usual readings first. The Devil is the illusion of materialism — not just over-attachment to money but the deeper claim that nothing exists beyond the senses. He perches on a block of stone that recalls the Emperor's cube, but only *half* a cube — incomplete knowledge. One hand up, one down, mirroring the Magician; but his torch points to earth instead of his wand to heaven. His palm bears Saturn (limitation, restriction). His open hand is the Hierophant's "more than meets the eye" gesture turned to mean "what you see is all there is". The reversed pentacle on his forehead — feet apart, arms out, the human body — places genitals above the head: desire over reason. The figures wear loose chains they could lift off any time; the calm on their faces is acceptance of a bad situation, the Devil's actual power being the illusion that no alternative exists. Many people only become consciously unhappy when they realise other lives are possible.

But Pollack doesn't stop at the moral reading. The Devil also stares out with great force, and the reversed pentacle pattern (sex organs above the head) recalls the Lovers — the woman looking up at the angel through her passion. Strength is directly above the Devil; the lion's energy raised and tamed. Tantric tradition calls this energy *kundalini*, fire at the base of the spine, evoked through sexual rite. The occult belief that sexual and spiritual force are one and the same is not so mysterious if you notice how much of ordinary human culture is built around sex; if the energy is that dominant, the path to enlightenment likely runs *through* it. Dreaming is always accompanied by genital arousal — the unconscious is sexual in nature, and dreams are partial transformations of it.

So the Devil is, in a wider sense, the life energy locked in the dark hidden areas of the self that cannot be reached by ordinary means. It is called the Devil because for the unprepared it manifests as monsters, the universe as evil, the temptation to violence and obsession. Paul Douglas calls trump 15 the "dark side of the collective unconscious". The black magician who conjures a demon is bringing out a force from inside the self; if mastered it becomes a servant, if not the magician becomes the slave. The third row requires a complete release of unconscious energy, and only Temperance has prepared the self enough to enter this territory without being broken by it — which is why the Greek patriarchs broke up the ecstatic mysteries: untrained worshippers, overwhelmed, tore through the countryside.

**In a reading.** The card usually carries its more obvious readings since it appears out of context. A narrow materialism. Misery, depression, the feeling of being chained or imprisoned with no alternative. Paired with the Lovers, a relationship begun in love that has turned into a trap. Being a slave to your desires rather than acting from what you believe is best — a controlling obsession, often sexual; the powerful attraction to someone you actively dislike. Shame, helplessness.

**Reversed.** The attempt to break loose from the bondage — real or psychological — beginning. The querent no longer accepts the situation. Paradoxically this is when the unhappiness is felt most strongly: you cannot slip the chains until you've seen them. Therapy, leaving home, a hard divorce — people in genuine liberation often feel worse than when they were merely accepting. Survival of this transition reshapes the personality; some slip back to the chains because the transition was unbearable. The reversed Devil in the position of past often means the situation has changed but the residual anger, sadness, depression remain. The psyche never simply lets anything go; the way through is to use the knowledge and energy bound up in every experience.
