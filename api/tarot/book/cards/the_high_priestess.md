# The High Priestess (II)

Originally called the Papess, the card carried the medieval legend of Pope Joan and the very real Sister Manfreda Visconti, burned by the Church in 1300 for being elected papess by a heretical sect — the same Visconti family then commissioned the first proper Tarot deck a century and a half later. Court de Gebelin renamed her the High Priestess and linked her to Isis; Waite's imagery follows that — the three-phase moon crown, the veil, the priestess's robe.

Pollack reads her as the dark, hidden half of the feminine archetype (the Empress carries the light, motherly half). She is the territory men have always projected onto women: mystery, the moon, the menstrual cycle, the warm dark womb, the seed in the winter earth, the wisdom that cannot be put into rational terms. Passivity here is not weakness — it is the condition under which reflection happens and the unconscious can speak. A culture obsessed with outer achievement fosters terror of this inner voice; without it, we can't know ourselves.

The two pillars carry the letters B and J — Boaz and Jakin, dark and light, action and stillness. The colours are reversed (a white B, a black J) the way the Tao dots cross over: each opposite already contains the other. Behind the veil between them lies only water — the unconscious itself, still on the surface, full underneath. Most of us cannot pass the pillars because we do not know how to go inward; the whole Major Arcana is the path to learning. The scroll in her lap is *Tora* (an anagram of *Taro*) — living truth available to us only in the distorted forms of myth and dream.

Ovid's Actaeon caught the moon-goddess naked, lingered too long, and was torn apart by his own dogs. The Fool's dance carries us past the High Priestess until we can return ready.

**In a reading.** A sense of mystery — things we do not know, things we cannot. A period of passive withdrawal that enriches an inner life. Intuition: knowing the answer without being able to say it. Visions, occult capacity, clairvoyance. At her most positive, strong potential we sense but have not yet acted on — action must still follow.

Negatively (depending on the surrounding cards), passivity held too long: weakness, fear of life, intuition that can't translate into engagement.

**Reversed.** A swing toward passion and outward involvement — sexual, emotional, competitive. If it swings too far, the loss of the inner sense that is the card's most precious gift.
