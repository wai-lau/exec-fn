# Temperance (XIV)

Below the Chariot. The Chariot was the ego built strong enough to deal with the world; over time that ego becomes rigid, behaviour turns from response into habit. The second row exists to free us from that artificial personality. Temperance is what comes out the other side: a person whose behaviour is reconnected to the real world but in a way more meaningful than the child's, because consciousness has been gained without being forfeited again. Spontaneity and knowledge held at once.

The Latin *temperare* means *to mix, to combine properly*. Most people hear "temperance" and think of moral self-control, but Pollack's Temperance does not go to extremes simply because extremes aren't necessary — not an inhibition, the opposite, a true response to each situation as it actually arises. Many people can only handle life by parcelling it: one personality for work and another for home, one register for serious moments and another for fun, the people they love often not the people they desire. Temperance dissolves the parcels.

Symbolism. The water poured between cups is the impossible flow — the lower cup not directly under the upper, the trick that looks magical to others because the Temperate person handles things with a joy that doesn't add up from the outside. Wirth makes the upper cup silver and the lower gold: moon to sun, unconscious to conscious. The two pillars of the earlier cards have become two mountains — abstract ideas turning into terrain. The road behind is the return: down into the self, now back to ordinary life, enriched. One foot on land, one in water — the Temperate self links the conscious world and the unconscious. The BOTA card pours water on a lion (Magician's fire) and torch-flames on an eagle (the higher form of Scorpio, Pollack's High Priestess principle); the basic duality fused. The angel resembles Iris, goddess of the rainbow — the peace after the flood, renewal after the death of old patterns. The triangle within the square: spirit rising from within ordinary embodied life. Look at the angel's collar — the Tetragrammaton, the four-letter name that was a fate-mystery on the Wheel, is now woven into the fabric. We become "masters" of fate by meeting life as it comes rather than running it through routines and defences.

**In a reading.** Moderation, balance, the middle path. Right action — doing the correct thing for the situation, which is very often *nothing*; the intemperate need to be doing something, and Temperance can show up as the antidote card to recklessness or hysteria. Mixing — blending disparate parts of a life into peace. In Minor-Arcana-heavy readings split between Wands and Cups (activity vs passivity) or Cups and Pentacles (fantasy vs reality), Temperance is the clue to bringing them together.

**Reversed.** Wildness, extremes, the inner sense of fit gone. The life has fragmented and the querent is sliding from one pole to another. Sometimes a warning that the death of old habits hasn't really happened. At simplest: calm down, avoid extremes. At deepest: the path leads back to Strength, to begin the long, sometimes frightening, finally joyous process of dying and being remade.
